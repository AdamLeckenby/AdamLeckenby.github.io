Laboratory Research

Having worked within research for nearly four years, I have built up experience with a range of techniques. These skills are predominantly within the field of molecular biology, in particular those relating to oncology, immunology, and genetics.

In addition to these skills, I regularly conduct immunohistochemistry, microscopy, and genetic work alongside further techniques in order to validate research results and further elucidate the underlying biology of disease.

Current Projects

  2. Immunohistochemistry - Imaging cellular localisation of PIM-1, PD-1, SOX3
  3. Single-cell Sequencing - Response to treatment in Fibroblast-like Synoviocytes
  4. Functional Studies - Flow Cytometry, Western Blotting, ELISA, HPLC, Imaging
  5. Experimental Studies - Retroviral Transduction, RNA silencing

Bioinformatics

In addition to my skills in the laboratory, I have gained much experience with bioinformatics. Whilst I predominantly program in R, I can also use coding languages such as Python capably, in addition to LaTeX, CSS, HTML, and Javascript.

Through the use of r/shiny, I can program webapps which researchers whom possess no programming or coding skills can adjust. This allows them to use slides, checkboxes, and other interactive elements to alter a graph or plot to their preference.

Furthermore, this allows higher-level bioinformatics to be utilised by researchers without requiring an understanding of coding -- this is particularly useful for researchers using mass cytometry which generates large, complex databases. I can enable them to perform t-SNE and SPADE analyses without the need to program.

You can view some of my research work (programmed in R) at the following website: adamleckenby.shinyapps.io/research/. This will take around fifteen seconds to render.
